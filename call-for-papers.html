Call for Papers

Important Dates

  • Submission deadline August 24, 2026
  • Notification of acceptance September 23, 2026
  • Camera-ready deadline September 30, 2026
  • Workshop date November 8, 2026

Topics of Interest

Submissions are welcome across—but not limited to—the following themes.

Generating Explanation Narratives

  • From artifacts to narratives: turning attributions, counterfactuals & saliency into language
  • Multimodal explanations (text, visuals, tables, code)
  • Agentic & multi-agent explanation pipelines
  • Causal and structured grounding of explanations
  • Narrative planning, composition & audience framing
  • Retrieval- and tool-augmented explanation generation

Trustworthy & Interpretable Explanations

  • Faithfulness and factual consistency of narratives
  • Detecting and mitigating hallucinated explanations
  • Mechanistic interpretability & circuit-level analysis
  • Aligning narratives with the model's actual reasoning
  • Evaluation: automatic metrics, human studies & benchmarks
  • Reproducibility and robustness

Human-Centered & Responsible XAI

  • Interactive and conversational explanations
  • Personalized, audience-adaptive explanations
  • Actionable recourse and human-in-the-loop decisions
  • High-stakes applications (healthcare, finance, public sector)
  • Auditing, compliance & regulation (EU AI Act, GDPR)
  • Responsible AI governance and risk

We encourage approaches grounded in structured explanation artifacts, rather than direct prompting of LLMs to generate post-hoc explanations without explicit XAI grounding.

Submission Guidelines

Paper length

  • Full papers — 10 pages excluding references; mature and validated research contributions, presented orally.
  • Short papers — 4 pages excluding references; preliminary, late-breaking, or exploratory work, presented as spotlights.
  • Both full and short papers may be accompanied by posters showcased during the coffee break.

Review process

  • Double-blind review with at least two Program Committee reviewers per submission.
  • Conflicts of interest and all review procedures strictly follow the official CIKM 2026 policies and guidelines.

Submission site

  • Papers are submitted via EasyChair — the link will be posted here once available.

Presentation

  • Full papers are presented orally; short papers as spotlights.
  • Both formats may include a poster to foster informal discussion.
